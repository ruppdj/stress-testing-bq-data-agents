### Golden State's Historic Home Dominance
The **Golden State Warriors** led the league by a wide margin, averaging **116.3 points per game** at home. This offensive firepower helped them achieve a nearly flawless home record of **39 wins and only 2 losses** (.951 win percentage) at Oracle Arena, which was a key component of their historic 73-9 regular season.

**SQL:**
```sql
SELECT
    gl.team_abbrev,
    ts.team AS team_name,
    ROUND(AVG(gl.pts), 1) AS avg_home_pts,
    SUM(CASE WHEN gl.wl = 'W' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS home_wins,
    SUM(CASE WHEN gl.wl = 'L' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS home_losses
FROM
    `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_game_logs` AS gl
INNER JOIN
    `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_team_season` AS ts
    ON gl.team_abbrev = ts.team_abbrev AND gl.season = ts.season
WHERE
    gl.season = 2016
    AND gl.is_home = TRUE
GROUP BY
    gl.team_abbrev,
    ts.team
ORDER BY
    avg_home_pts DESC
LIMIT 10
```

### Key Insights

```sql
WITH traded AS (
    SELECT player, season 
    FROM `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_player_stats`
```

### P1-24 — Among players on an NBA championship team, who had the biggest drop in PER the following season? Only count players who appeared in at least 10 games in both seasons.

**Expected:** Bill Walton: 1986 BOS champ, PER 17.0 -> 5.6 (-11.4); threshold is >= 10 (Walton's 1987 = exactly 10 g)

**Response:** To identify the players on an NBA championship team who experienced the largest drop in Player Efficiency Rating (PER) the following season, we analyzed individual player statistics for both the championship year and the subsequent season. 

### Criteria:
1. **Championship Winner:** Identified using the playoff series winners (`round = 'Finals'`).
2. **Team Association:** Only players who played for the championship team during their winning season were counted.
3. **Minimum Game Cutoff:** Players must have appeared in at least 10 games in both the championship season and the following season.
4. **Drop Calculation:** Calculated as `PER (Championship Season) - PER (Following Season)`.

Here are the top players with the biggest declines in PER after winning a championship:

### Key Insights:

* **Elden Campbell** experienced the largest drop in PER in the dataset after winning a championship. After winning the title with the **Detroit Pistons in 2004**, his PER dropped by **15.2** from a solid 13.8 to -1.4 in the 2004-05 season.

**SQL:**
```sql
WITH champs AS (
  SELECT season, series_winner AS champion_team
  FROM `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_playoff_series`
  WHERE round = 'Finals'
),
player_season_stats AS (
  -- Get overall season stats for each player
  SELECT 
    player,
    season,
    per,
    g,
    team_abbrev
  FROM `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_player_stats`
  WHERE is_traded_player = true
  
  UNION ALL
  
  SELECT 
    p.player,
    p.season,
    p.per,
    p.g,
    p.team_abbrev
  FROM `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_player_stats` p
  WHERE p.is_traded_player = false
    AND NOT EXISTS (
      SELECT 1 
      FROM `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_player_stats` t
      WHERE t.player = p.player 
        AND t.season = p.season 
        AND t.is_traded_player = true
    )
),
players_on_champs AS (
  SELECT DISTINCT p.player, c.season, c.champion_team
  FROM `nba-data-agent-testing.nba_marts.mart_player_stats` p
  JOIN champs c 
    ON p.season = c.season 
   AND p.team_abbrev = c.champion_team
  WHERE p.is_traded_player = false
)
SELECT 
  pc.player,
  pc.season AS champ_season,
  pc.champion_team,
  s1.per AS champ_season_per,
  s1.g AS champ_season_games,
  (pc.season + 1) AS next_season,
  s2.per AS next_season_per,
  s2.g AS next_season_games,
  ROUND(s1.per - s2.per, 2) AS per_drop
FROM players_on_champs pc
JOIN player_season_stats s1 
  ON pc.player = s1.player 
 AND pc.season = s1.season
JOIN player_season_stats s2 
  ON pc.player = s2.player 
 AND (pc.season + 1) = s2.season
WHERE s1.g >= 10 
  AND s2.g >= 10
ORDER BY per_drop DESC
LIMIT 10;

```
