### P1-08 — How has league average pace changed from the 1994-95 season to the 2018-19 season?

**Expected:** ~92.9 (1995) -> ~100.0 (2019), U-shape; trough 88.92 (1999 lockout) or ~90.9 mid-2000s both acceptable

**Response:** To analyze how the league average pace changed from the 1994-95 season (`season = 1995`) to the 2018-19 season (`season = 2019`), we computed the average team pace across all franchises for each season. 

The data reveals a distinct U-shaped curve in how fast NBA teams played over this 25-year period.

### NBA Pace Evolution Insights

1. **The Late 90s Slowdown**: In the 1994-95 season, the league average pace was **92.91**. It steadily declined over the next few years, hitting a historic low in the strike-shortened 1998-99 season at **88.92**—reflecting a heavily half-court, defense-oriented era.
2. **The Era of Stabilization (2000–2013)**: For over a decade, the average pace remained remarkably stable, generally hovering between **90.0** and **93.0** possessions per 48 minutes.
3. **The "Pace and Space" Explosion (2014–2019)**: Starting in the 2013-14 season, the league underwent a dramatic transformation. WITH top_vorp AS (

    player,

    vorp,
